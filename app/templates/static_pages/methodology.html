Methodology

This page explains how Veritas analyzes news data, groups incidents, calculates credibility, and presents structured insights.

Design Philosophy

Veritas follows a rule-based, explainable analysis model. Every metric shown on the platform is derived from transparent logic rather than opaque machine learning systems.

  • No sentiment analysis
  • No opinion scoring
  • No automated editorial judgments
  • No black-box machine learning models

The goal is to assist understanding, not to influence viewpoints.

1. Data Collection

News articles are collected from predefined and verified news sources. Each source is stored with associated metadata such as category and reliability.

  • Articles are fetched using APIs or structured feeds
  • Duplicate articles are removed
  • Articles are normalized for consistent storage

Only publicly available news content is used.

2. Incident Grouping

Individual news articles are grouped into a single incident when they describe the same real-world event.

Grouping is based on:

  • Incident type (e.g. protest, accident, policy decision)
  • Geographic location
  • Time window (± defined number of days)

A many-to-many relationship is maintained between incidents and news articles.

3. Credibility Scoring

Each incident receives a credibility score between 0 and 100. The score reflects the structural reliability of reporting, not truthfulness or intent.

Scoring Factors:

  • Source Diversity (40%)
    Incidents reported by multiple independent sources receive higher scores.
  • Location Clarity (30%)
    Clear, consistent geographic information increases credibility.
  • Completeness of Information (30%)
    Presence of dates, locations, descriptions, and key details.

Credibility scoring is deterministic and reproducible.

4. Perspective Distribution Analysis

Veritas categorizes news coverage based on the source type, not the content or tone of the article.

Perspective Categories:

  • Public – citizen-focused, community-impact reporting
  • Neutral – factual, balanced reporting
  • Political – policy, governance, or government-focused reporting

The system counts how many articles from each category contribute to an incident and displays the percentage distribution.

Veritas does not analyze opinions, emotions, or sentiment.

5. Similar Incident Identification

To provide historical context, Veritas identifies similar past incidents.

Matching criteria include:

  • Incident type similarity
  • Geographic proximity
  • Temporal proximity (within a defined time range)

This allows users to recognize recurring patterns and trends.

6. Personalized Recommendations

Veritas provides personalized recommendations for logged-in users.

  • User reading history is tracked
  • Frequently viewed categories and locations are identified
  • Recent related news is recommended

Recommendations are rule-based and privacy-conscious.

System Flow Summary

  1. News articles are collected and stored
  2. Articles are grouped into incidents
  3. Analysis modules compute structured metrics
  4. Results are cached for performance
  5. Frontend visualizes the analysis

Limitations & Transparency

While Veritas provides structured analysis, it does not claim to determine absolute truth.

  • Analysis depends on available sources
  • Source classification is predefined
  • Edge cases may require human interpretation

Veritas emphasizes transparency, structure, and repeatability over subjective interpretation.
